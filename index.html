💚 What to Eat — Hormone-Happy Foods
🫘 Dal every meal🥬 Palak & Methi 🍚 Brown rice (¼ cup)🥚 Eggs 🐟 Fish🍗 Chicken (not fried) 🫐 Berries & Papaya🥛 Curd & Buttermilk 🎃 Pumpkin Seeds🥜 Walnuts 🌱 Flaxseeds💛 Turmeric 🌾 Jowar & Bajra🧄 Garlic & Ginger
🥗 Best Vegetables
  • Palak — iron + calcium for PCOD
  • Methi — lowers insulin resistance
  • Lauki — near zero calories, fills up
  • Bhindi — fiber, helps blood sugar
  • Gobhi (cooked) — anti-inflammatory
🍲 Dal & Legumes
  • Moong dal — easiest to digest
  • Masoor dal — iron + folate
  • Chana dal — best for blood sugar
  • Rajma — complete protein with rice
  • Sprouted moong — raw superfood
💡 Golden rule: Fill 40% with sabzi + salad FIRST. Then dal. Then a tiny scoop of rice. Never eat rice alone!

🚫 What NOT to Eat — Strictly Avoid These
🍬 Sugar & Sweets🍞 Maida 📦 Packaged Food🥛 Excess Dairy 🌱 Soya (excess)🍺 Alcohol 🥤 Cold Drinks🍟 Fried Food ☕ Coffee near medicine
Why Sugar Is Enemy #1
  • Causes sharp insulin spikes
  • Feeds androgen production
  • Promotes belly weight gain
  • Disrupts your period cycle
  • Switch to tiny jaggery or skip
Why Limit Regular Dairy
  • Regular milk has growth hormones
  • Can raise androgens in PCOD
  • Curd & buttermilk = OKAY ✅
  • Switch to almond or oat milk
  • Small paneer 2–3x/week fine
⚠️ Soya alert: Soya mimics estrogen. For PCOD & thyroid girls, daily soya disrupts already imbalanced hormones. Occasional use okay, not daily!

🍚 Which Rice? The Real Talk

✅ Pick These

  • 🌾 Brown rice — more fiber, lower GI
  • 🌾 Hand-pounded rice — less processed
  • 🌾 Red / Matta rice — antioxidant-rich
  • 🌾 ¼ cup white rice — always with dal

❌ Limit These

  • 🍚 Large plate white rice alone
  • 🍚 Polished basmati in big portions
  • 🍚 Rice alone at dinner
💡 Real talk: You do NOT need to quit rice! Keep it to ¼ cup (20% of plate) — always with dal. Brown rice is better but small white rice + dal is fine.
🌾 Best swap: Replace white rice 3–4 days/week with khichdi — low GI, high protein, ultimate PCOD comfort meal! 🥰

💪 Protein Intake — How Much & From Where
🎯 For a 20-year-old girl with PCOD: aim for 55–70g protein/day. Protein balances hormones, reduces cravings, supports thyroid & keeps you glowy 💕
Protein in everyday Indian foods:
🥚 2 boiled eggs12g
🫘 1 cup moong dal (cooked)9g
🍗 100g chicken breast27g
🐟 100g fish22g
🥛 ½ cup curd5g
🌱 1 cup sprouted moong7g
🌿 Vegetarian Sources
  • Dal every meal — non-negotiable
  • Curd + buttermilk daily
  • Pumpkin + flaxseeds snack
  • Sprouted moong chaat
  • Small paneer 2–3x/week
🥩 Non-Veg Sources
  • 2 eggs daily — best value!
  • Grilled or boiled chicken
  • Steamed or pan-fried fish
  • Avoid fried versions always
  • Fish 2–3x/week for omega-3
🥤 Should You Use Protein Powder?
⚠️ Important: Most protein powders are NOT designed for hormonal conditions. Food protein is always better than powder.

✅ Safer Options

  • Pure whey isolate — low lactose
  • Pea protein — hormone-neutral
  • Brown rice protein — PCOD-safe
  • 1 scoop max/day with water

🚫 Avoid These

  • Soy protein — mimics estrogen!
  • Powders with added sugar
  • Mass gainers — bad for PCOD
  • Any soy lecithin base powder

⚠️ Caution — Don't Use Excess

  • Excess powder → kidney stress & liver load
  • Too much whey → acne & hormonal flare-up
  • If thyroid patient — consult Dr. Divya first
💡 Bottom line: If your dal + eggs + curd are regular, you likely don't need powder at all. Always check with your doctor first! 🌸
